            Stage one. The shell.
            
The empty room.

            
Before a single panel goes up, we measure the geometry, the reflections, the standing waves — and design backwards from the room that needs to exist.

          

          

            Stage two. Acoustic treatment.
            
The room stops fighting itself.

            
Bass traps in the corners. Broadband absorbers at the first reflection points. Diffusers on the back wall.

          

          

            Stage three. Speakers, 7.2.4.
            
A speaker for every direction.

            
Left, center, right. Side surrounds. Rear. Four height channels for Atmos. Two subs, calibrated by SPL meter, not by ear.

          

          

            Stage four. Image.
            
A screen that vanishes.

            
Native 4K laser projection. Acoustically transparent screen. ISF-calibrated grayscale, primaries, gamma. The black bars disappear. The screen disappears. The film stays.

          

          

            Stage five. Light and shade.
            
Five scenes. One button.

            
Lutron full-spectrum tunable LEDs in the cove and recessed cans. Motorized blackout shades. Day. Reveal. Dim. Feature. House Up.

          

          

            Stage six. Tuned.
            
Eight seats. One sweet spot.

            
Tiered seating. Front row at the calibrated mix position. Every room takes the time it takes. Calibrated, not configured.
